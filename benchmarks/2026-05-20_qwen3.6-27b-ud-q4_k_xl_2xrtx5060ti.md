# unsloth/Qwen3.6-27B-MTP-GGUF (UD-Q4_K_XL) on 2× RTX 5060 Ti — **OOM at model load**

Follow-up to #4 ([source post claiming ~55% faster UD-XL quants on 5090](https://x.com/...)) — does the UD-Q4_K_XL variant beat plain Q4_K_M on our 2× 5060 Ti rig?

**Outcome**: not on 2× 16 GB. MTP context fails to allocate compute buffers.

## Command

```bash
./bench.py https://huggingface.co/unsloth/Qwen3.6-27B-MTP-GGUF \
    --gpu rtx-5060ti --num-gpus 2 \
    --bin llama-cli --quant UD-Q4_K_XL \
    --params "--spec-draft-p-min 0.75 --spec-type draft-mtp"
```

## Result

## Diagnosis

UD-Q4_K_XL is 800 MB larger than the plain Q4_K_M we used successfully on
2026-05-19. The model+KV cache+spec-decode compute buffer pushes GPU 1 over
16 GB. The original Q4_K_M run on this same hardware also hit a cudaMalloc
warning during compute-buffer alloc but managed to recover (smaller buffer
on retry); the XL variant doesn't get that headroom.

## Next steps

- Lower context (`-c 16384` instead of llama-cli default) to shrink KV cache
  and free room for the MTP compute buffer.
- Or accept that **UD-Q4_K_XL needs ≥ 20 GB single-card VRAM** to run with
  MTP — i.e. the source's 5090 result is the floor configuration.
- A direct 2× 5060 Ti apples-to-apples comparison vs the source's 5090 isn't
  available without going to a smaller quant.
